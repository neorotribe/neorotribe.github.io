Taronga Zoo

By June Ong
September 19, 2020
0

Everytime we visit Taronga Zoo, our experience is always different, in a good way. The view of the harbour would almost always guarantee a mood lift. Today we got there around lunch time. Kids were excited as usual. They are pretty straight forward, they are happy when they get to go free range. 

I was just a little nervous when we saw the line to the seal show. It was a massive queue and there weren't much social distancing. We made our way against the traffic of people and made a beeline to the playground. I think any person that has gone to Taronga enough with food can tell you the resident turkeys there are relentless. They are really aggressive and no longer afraid of humans. The whole stress about guarding your children's food became like a chore really. We ended up force-feeding our children at double the speed while wary-eyed of the turkeys the whole time. I supposed tourists would find this fascinating and wouldn't mind the experience of kookaburras swopping down on your fish-and-chips or turkeys ransacking your snackbag. 

And a heads up, the usual main restaurant near the elephant enclosure is open for takeaway but not for sit down dining. BYOF. And maybe a red hot chilli and feed it to the turkey when it gets too annoying (I am kidding!). 

If you fancy a good exercise, you will work your glutes in the zoo walking up and down the slopes. It is especially intense on the way out to the exit. This is why I got my 9 yo Liam to push the stroller up today. Return of Investment. HA HA.

I still think the zoo is a relatively safe place for a family day out in regards to the Covid situation. There are plenty of greens and spots for a sit down picnic. Some animals enclosures are pretty small and it does get a little crowded for my liking. But as parents it is on us to practice common sense and remind our kids about keeping their distance and maintaining their hygiene. 

Taronga Zoo also made provision for the autism community. Guests with autism and their family and friends get to enjoy early entry to the zoo on select days in the year. There is also an option of the zoo team providing extra assistance should there be a need. Read more here.

Due to the current COVID-19 safety restrictions, all visitors are required to purchase tickets online (with up to 20% savings online!) and attend the Zoo on their selected date only. Tickets will not be available for purchase at the gate. If you plan to visit Taronga Zoo this season, you are advised to book online prior to attending. This is to prevent overcrowding and ensure social distancing measures can be effective. 

Enjoy your Taronga Zoo visit!
