Vtech Kidizoom Duo Camera

June Ong September 22, 2020 Reviews

Lily received her first birthday present this year from her Grandma in Singapore. She asked for a pink camera because she loves pink for everything and she was coveting after Levi's camera for a while now.

We combed through the web and realised Vtech Kidizoom Duo Camera has received raving reviews above the rest. It is on the pricier side but we wanted to get something that is durable and educational. Vtech has a good reputation of creating toys that has educational value. We have had their toys for both the boys before and they played with them for a long time. 

The camera has a front and rear photo-taking function. It is up to date for the selfies-wefies generation. It has built-in auto-flash, large colour screen, 4x digital zoom and also voice recorder with voice changing effects and built-in games. The games were something I wasn't keen on. Lily tried to play with them but she was too young for it. She ended up throwing a tantrum which was too familiar in my household. My kids has a bad rep of throwing tantrums over screens, ipads and technology. 

I wasn't keen on Lily or the boys hogging on the camera for the games. The problem with my kids at the moment is once they get on the screen, they get stimulated to the point where any other activity seems boring to them. So I just didn't want to dig myself another hole. 

Anyway, Brandon discovered that the games can be disabled. So it comes with parental control for that. Well done VTech, for considering the luddites mum like me.

We disabled the game function and let the kids use the camera as it is. And Lily has used it to take many unflattering photos of me, which I will spare you the agony of them.

Overall, it is a good buy and investment. It is a good present for kids as well. It is recommended for ages 3-9 so I can see it being used across my kids who happens to be age 3, 6 and 9.
