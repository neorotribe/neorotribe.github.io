Coffee Trad3rs

June Ong September 22, 2020 Reviews

Our good friend has recommended Coffee Trad3rs to us sometimes back. The owner, James, a Taiwanese has a wealth of experience in the cafe industry. His previous stint was in Malaysia helping a friend to set up a cafe. 

We decided to hit the cafe during Father's Day weekend. To be honest, we weren't sure about moving to Hills District but we have been pleasantly surprised by the community so far. There is a good mix of nature and shopping districts littered everywhere. People are alot laid back here, and always happy to strike up a convo. 

Upon entering the cafe, you would be overwhelmed by the number of indoor plants who are thriving and happy. James is the main vibe of the cafe. He is chirpy, loud and engaging. You could see straightaway that he is gifted to do this.

The coffee and food were phenomenal. There was a Father's Day special, pork knuckle. I wasn't keen on the meat so I devoured the rest of the vegetables. Every single dish tasted differently and you could tell care and thought were put into preparing them. 

We had the 3 kids with us and it is always a challenge to keep them occupied. Levi would get bored very easily and he would lead the pack to be silly. We have to juggle to keep them seated and happy before they embark on this slippery road of no return (Pun intended). There were so many times we have to leave and return home. Stories for another day.

James was very attentive and kept coming back to the table to engage the kids. He promised them ice-cream (The cafe has their own gelato chest!). Mind you, it was a full house indoor and outdoor. James was not just interested to earn your bucks, he was about heart and making friendship. We left the place with our hearts full that day. 

You can't counterfeit heart and sincerity in doing business. People can tell. 

Anyway, it is definitely worth a visit if you are in the area. Good coffee and good food. The setting is serene if it's not busy but buzzing with life it is. Look out for James, he might give you a gelato.

Coffee Traders
Shop 1 / 8 Victoria Avenue,
Castle Hill, NSW
Mon-Fri: 6am – 4pm
Sat: 7:30am – 3:30pm
Sun: Closed
