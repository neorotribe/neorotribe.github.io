Authenticity = Happiness

By June
10 September 2020
0

This year I gained a valuable piece of wisdom. I wanted to be happier, to be more present and to be more authentic.

You know as a parent you can get away with saying anything when your kids are young. They do not know any better nor judge you. Now that my kids are older, they would often retort back. "Why do you say stupid, that is not nice" or "Why do you whinge when you ask me not to?" You just can't have double standards anymore. 

And quitting the double standards actually inched me forward to be a more authentic and happier individual. There used to be a disparity in what I say and how I feel. It is probably a learnt behaviour growing up. I would say what I don't mean. I have mastered a skill of saying what people wants to hear. I have quit flattery so that was not an issue for me in recent times. 

Let me give you a scenario to illustrate my point better.

Let's say I were to help a fellow friend with moving home. I appear to be willing and empathetic to my friend but complained about how tired it was to my husband after. I realised this disparity in between has caused me my happiness and my reliability with my children. So I decided to close this disparity gap for good.

If I were to say something, I stay consistent in my word and thoughts. I quit being flippant or fickle. This has also helped me to stop overpromising and underdelivering in my life in general. I could write a thousand essays on this alone.

Saying what you mean and meaning what you say happens to be the golden mantra of Brandon's too. He talks about it all the time with the kids. He is such an amazing human and the kids know he follow through what he promises each time. This is also why threatening our children never works and always end up backfiring on us. We all strive to be good reliable dependable parents to our children and one of the most important thing is to follow through our words, in punishment or in rewards. 

What words do you use? What would you say? Do you find that being authentic is to stay consistent to your words in front and behind the scenes?
