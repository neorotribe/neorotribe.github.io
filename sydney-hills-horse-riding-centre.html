Sydney Hills Horse Riding Centre

June Ong September 24, 2020 Reviews

Staying in the Hills has worked out really well for us. We are close to every possible option of outdoor activity. I have often meet parents who either has horses as pets or their kids riding as a hobby. There is also much traction on equine therapy, how horses are used as a form of therapy for autistic children. 

The funny thing is, Brandon was nearly killed by a horse. When he was 14, a horse riding accident left him hanging for life at the edge of a cliff in a quarry. That's heaps of drama within a sentence. I carried the perspective of how a horse can be erratic and dangerous from his experience. 

But you know me, I have to be convinced with my own experience. I never settled with a hearsay. Our belief as parents is to allow our children to grow from different kind of experiences. So getting our kids to take up horse riding is an inevitable thing. I am also secretly hoping through the kids, Brandon would find closure with his traumatic incident.

We booked in a 30 mins session for Lily. We were told that children grow with the horses. Lily was very easy and engaging. She was given a crash course on brushing and saddling Cloud, the shetland pony. It was more on getting Lily to be comfortable around him. I was a little concerned with Lily's eczema flare up as Cloud was massively shedding. But Lily was alright for the day. Apparently horses grow thick coats during Winter and shed them coming Spring and Summer. How amazing is nature.

The owner Carolyn took over the second half of the lesson. We saddled Lily on Cloud and went for a ride around the vicinity. Lily was taught how to move and stop Cloud. I must say for a 30 mins lesson, it was ample for Lily to gain a good experience with horse riding. She was really exhausted by the end of it. We were quite happy with how it all turned out. 

We have signed both Liam and Levi for their holiday camps coming in Oct. It would be really interested to see how it would pen out for Liam because he can not tolerate strong smell. Perhaps the calming effect or a budding love for a horse could help him overcome his sensory irregularities. 

Sydney Hills Horse Riding Centre is in the midst of getting approval to become an NDIS provider. This is a piece of good news as this would mean special needs family in Hills District would enjoy the close proximity to the centre. 

What I love about them is their horses enjoy 2-3 days of break in the week. This commitment of caring for their horses would definitely transcend in the service and work they do. This is something we want to instil in our children, caring for their environment, nature and animals.
