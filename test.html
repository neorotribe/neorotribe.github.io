Let's Rewild Ourselves

By June
9 September 2020
0

I was a typical Singaporean. I was a city girl. I love efficiency, cleanliness, structure, and on. With all these expectations came a certain level of obnoxiousness. I expected much of myself and everyone. It was a straightforward equation in life for me; if you grow apple seeds, you reap apple fruits out of an apple tree. 

If only life was that simple. 

I thought hard and long on why we are and who we are now. Then I realised our kids made us. I say it all the time, my kids do not belong to an orderly civilisation. They are just wild. They need space, stimulation, walls to bounce off from and to be occupied with things to do. They get bored easily, their minds are like deep recesses created to suck every ounce of energy and blood of their caretakers. Please don't be mistaken, they are great kids. They just need their sufficient fill of exploration and learning daily. 

If we suppressed their need for exploration with screen time, they become restless and agitated. Liam had unrestricted access to the iPad last summer holidays and I wouldn't even go into detail the aftermath of it. (Well, I actually did in an previous post.) Let's just say we lost a child. He was disengaged and uninterested with life because he was overstimulated. 

And so Brandon and I resigned to the fact that we had to go on a rewild and rewire journey as a family. We haven't had a TV for the past 5 years. We have no iPad for the past year. That said, the children do have access to movies on the laptop during the weekends or when we have visitors. I am not selling our way of living; we just had to do it because that was the best way for us. But I have to say every family can look into regulating their children's access to technology and internet. It is worth talking about it, fighting against the norm and working it out as a family. 

Tell me your thoughts and struggles below. I am all ears!
